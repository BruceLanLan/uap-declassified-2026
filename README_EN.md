# U.S. Department of War UAP/UFO Declassified Documents Analysis

[English Version](README_EN.md) | [中文版本](README.md)

This repository contains a detailed analysis of the first batch of UAP (Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena) archives declassified by the U.S. Department of War on May 8, 2026. All raw metadata and key contents were extracted and structured from `war.gov/UFO`.

## 📁 Repository Contents

*   **`uap_metadata.csv`**: The complete metadata inventory of 161 declassified files, including video descriptions, image narratives, NASA astronaut transcripts, and FBI reports.
*   **`README.md`**: Analysis report in Chinese.
*   **`README_EN.md`**: Analysis report in English.

## 🔍 Core Findings and Archive Classification

Based on the analysis of the declassified CSV file, the 161 documents are primarily divided into the following categories:

### 1. Military Operations & Video Records

The U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM), Indo-Pacific Command (INDOPACOM), and Africa Command (AFRICOM) submitted numerous UAP videos captured by military infrared sensors (FLIR) and full-motion video (FMV) cameras.

*   **Typical Characteristics**: Many videos show high-contrast anomalous areas ("areas of contrast"). These objects often exhibit irregular trajectories, extremely high speeds, or hover in mid-air. Some objects were described as an "inverted teardrop," a "glowing white ball of light," or an "eight-pointed star."
*   **Key Cases**:
    *   **Greece, October 2023**: A UAP was recorded flying near the ocean surface, making multiple "90-degree turns" at approximately 80 mph.
    *   **Middle East, 2020**: Infrared sensors captured fast-moving anomalous signals that suddenly disappeared.

### 2. FBI Sighting Reports & Images

The FBI submitted numerous still images extracted from U.S. government systems (e.g., FBI Photo A1-A8, B1-B24 series) and witness interview records (FBI 302 reports).

*   **"Western US Event"**: This is one of the most compelling cases in the archive. In 2023, seven federal law enforcement special agents witnessed multiple UAP phenomena over two days, including:
    *   **"Orbs Launching Orbs"**: An orange "mother orb" releasing smaller red orbs in the sky before disappearing.
    *   **"Large, Fiery Orb"**: A glowing orange orb, approximately 12-18 meters in diameter, hovering near a rock pinnacle. It was described as "similar to the Eye of Sauron from Lord of the Rings."
    *   **"Dark Kite" & "Transparent Kite"**: Agents pursued a triangular/kite-shaped object moving with "zero resistance" in the pre-dawn hours.
*   **2025 Test Site Event**: During a helicopter search, senior intelligence officials and pilots encountered a "super-hot" orb that outpaced their helicopter. It later split into multiple glowing orbs performing complex formation flights.

### 3. NASA Historical Archives (Apollo & Skylab)

The declassified files include multiple crew communication transcripts and technical debriefings from the Apollo missions and Skylab, confirming anomalous sightings by astronauts in space.

*   **Apollo 11 (1969)**: Buzz Aldrin reported observing an object with a "sizeable dimension" on the way to the Moon.
*   **Apollo 12 (1969)**: Alan Bean described observing glowing particles and flashes of light "escaping the Moon" through the optical telescope. The archive also contains several marked photographs of lunar surface anomalies (NASA-UAP-VM1 to VM5).
*   **Apollo 17 (1972)**: Eugene Cernan and Harrison Schmitt reported intense flashes, rotating phenomena, and anomalous flashes on the lunar surface.
*   **Skylab (1973-1974)**: Multiple crews reported flashing lights outside the window and an unusually bright object with a "reddish hue."

### 4. State Department Cables

These documents record international UAP events collected by U.S. embassies abroad.

*   **Papua New Guinea (1985)**: Local intelligence reported high-altitude, high-speed aircraft causing public panic, with radar capturing the anomalous targets.
*   **Kazakhstan (1994)**: Pilots encountered an extremely bright UAP at 41,000 feet. The object executed 90-degree turns and corkscrew maneuvers at immense speeds, described as resembling a "bullet in flight."

## 📝 Conclusion

This batch of archives declassified by the U.S. Department of War provides the first centralized presentation of UAP evidence collected by the military, intelligence agencies, and space exploration departments. Although most reports emphasize that "video descriptions are for informational purposes only," these records—spanning decades and provided by highly credible witnesses (astronauts, special agents, and military pilots) alongside physical sensor data—offer significant support for the reality of UAP phenomena. In particular, the cross-corroboration by multiple agents in the "Western US Event" demonstrates that UAPs possess flight characteristics far exceeding known aviation technologies.
