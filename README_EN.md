## 🔑 Highlight Cases

<details>
<summary><b>🟠 "Orbs Launching Orbs" — FBI Western U.S. Event (2026)</b></summary>

Seven federal agents independently witnessed orange "mother orbs" releasing smaller red orbs at dusk in the Western U.S. over two days from multiple vantage points. AARO later measured the object at approximately **12–18 meters in diameter**, ~**1,050 meters** from observers, hovering with "zero resistance."

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>🟢 Aegean Sea UAP — Military Mission Report (2023)</b></summary>

U.S. military pilots on a mission over the Aegean Sea captured an unidentified spherical object on infrared sensors performing **90-degree turns** beyond fighter aircraft maneuvering limits, with no heat signature or visible propulsion.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>🟠 Apollo 11 — Astronaut Technical Debriefing (1969)</b></summary>

Buzz Aldrin and crew described observing unidentified luminous objects en route to the Moon, and documented unexplained flashes on the lunar surface. This is the first officially declassified astronaut UAP sighting record.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>🟣 Kazakhstan State Dept. Cable (1994)</b></summary>

A Kazakhstani pilot witnessed a UAP performing a **90-degree turn at 41,000 feet**. The U.S. Embassy reported the incident to the State Department in a cable now declassified.

</details>

## ℹ️ About This Repository

**Source**: [U.S. Department of War PURSUE Program](https://www.war.gov/UFO/), Release 01 — May 8, 2026

**Background**: President Trump posted on Truth Social on February 19, 2026, directing the Department of War and relevant agencies to begin identifying, reviewing, declassifying, and publicly releasing UAP-related government records. This is the largest UAP declassification effort in U.S. history, involving dozens of agencies and tens of millions of records.

**File Classification**: All records are designated as "Unresolved Cases" — the government cannot make a definitive determination on the nature of the observed phenomena, often due to insufficient data. The Department of War welcomes private-sector analysis of these materials.

**Disclaimer**: This repository is for archival and research purposes only. All files are the property of the U.S. Government.
